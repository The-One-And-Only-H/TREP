Christopher Isherwood

Christopher Isherwood (1904-1986) was the author of Goodbye to Berlin (1939) and A Single Man (1964). As a teenager, he churned out stories and poems, created a literary fantasy world with is friend Edward Upward, and wrote plays with WH Auden. Asked to leave Cambridge University after deliberately ruining his exam paper, Isherwood spent a brief spell studying medicine before heading off to Weimar, Berlin. Years later, in Christopher and His Kind (1976), Isherwood revealed the story of a contemporary and sexually accomplished queer hero. Goodbye to Berlin was reworked by John Van Druten for the stage play I Am a Camera (1951), which was later adapted into the hugely successful musical Cabaret (1966). Isherwood's fame grew, but by 1953, he had other things on his mind and fell in love with 18 year old college student, Don Bachardy. After a tricky patch that inspired Isherwood's A Single Man, one of his finest works, the pair stayed together until Isherwood's death in 1986.
